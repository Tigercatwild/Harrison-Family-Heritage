Our Family History

Our Origins

John (originally Juan) Rufus Harrison, Sr., a native of Cuba, came to Florida in the 1800s where he met and married Mary Jones. From this union one daughter, Mary, and two sons, John Rufus, Jr., and Ronia were born.

Early Settlement

John Rufus, Sr. settled in Jacksonville, Florida. After he and his wife, Mary passed away, their three young children were taken in by a cousin, Edward Jones, Sr., and his wife Jane, of Lake City, Florida, where they raised the children on a farm in the Falling Creek area of Columbia County, Florida.

Family Growth

As adults, John Rufus Harrison, Jr. married Millie Bryant and had nine children. Ronia Harrison, Sr. married Hattie Welcome and had eight children. These families formed the foundation of our extensive family tree that continues to grow today.
